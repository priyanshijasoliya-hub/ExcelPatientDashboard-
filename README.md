📌 Project Overview
This project features an interactive Excel Data Dashboard designed to analyze patient demographics, health behaviors, and healthcare facility utilization. By synthesizing clinical metrics (such as BMI and smoking habits) with financial expenditures,
this dashboard provides actionable insights into public health trends and healthcare cost drivers across various regional facilities.
The core objective is to identify regional health patterns, evaluate the economic impact of specific health conditions, and highlight facilities with disproportionate expenditure to assist in strategic healthcare decision-making.

📊 Key Features & Functionality
Dynamic Data Slicing: Fully interactive filters for Sex, Hospital Level (e.g., County, National Referral), Weight Status (Underweight to Obese), and Geographic Region to drill down into specific cohorts.

Multi-Dimensional Analysis: Cross-references patient lifestyle choices (smoking) against regional distribution and individual financial impacts.

Facility Mapping: Utilizes tree-mapping and detailed horizontal bar charts to visualize patient volume and track facility-specific cost variations.

📈 Dashboard Insights & Outcomes
Based on the dataset analysis of 115 patients (with an overall average age of ~38.6 years and an average BMI of ~30.8), the dashboard reveals the following key outcomes:

1. Smoking Patterns & Regional Concentration
Observation: While non-smokers (85) significantly outnumber smokers (30), the distribution is highly uneven across regions.

Outcome: The majority of smokers are concentrated in the Southeast region. This clearly pinpoints the geographic focus area needed for targeted public health interventions and smoking cessation programs.

2. Healthcare Cost Drivers & Facility Disparities
Observation: Total expenditure varies heavily by region, with the Southwest and Southeast leading in total financial load.

Outcome: On an individual level, Westlands Sub-County Hospital and Kibuye Healthcentre record the highest average healthcare charges (exceeding $23,000–$25,000 per patient). 
This highlights an immediate need for operational audits to investigate what unique factors or specialized treatments are driving costs up in these specific facilities.

3. BMI and Weight Status Correlations
Observation: The average patient BMI sits at 30.7977, pushing the general baseline into the "Obese" category.

Outcome: Patients with higher BMI values are predominantly classified as obese, with a specific cohort average peak at 34.975. This underscores a widespread prevalence of obesity within the monitored population,
signaling a critical need for targeted weight management and wellness initiatives.
